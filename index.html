— FREQUENTLY ASKED —

Got Questions?

What is Siphonic Roof Drainage?+
Siphonic Roof Drainage is an innovative system designed to efficiently and rapidly remove rainwater from roofs using the principle of siphon action. It allows for optimal drainage even at low flow rates, making it a highly effective solution for a wide variety of roof structures.
How does Siphonic drainage differ from traditional systems?+
Unlike traditional gravity-based drainage, siphonic systems use a network of pipes to create a vacuum — enabling faster, more efficient water removal, especially over long horizontal distances, and with significantly fewer downpipes and outlets.
What types of roofs are suitable for Siphonic drainage?+
Siphonic drainage is versatile and suitable for flat roofs, curved roofs, industrial buildings, commercial developments, airports, and more. Its adaptability makes it an excellent choice across a wide range of architectural designs.
What are the main advantages of Siphonic Roof Drainage?+
Key benefits include efficient water removal, space optimisation, cost-effectiveness, reduced material use, lower CO₂ footprint, and minimal maintenance. Particularly advantageous for large roof areas and complex building structures.
How does Siphonic Innovations ensure project success?+
Our success lies in a comprehensive approach combining over 5 years of hydraulic engineering experience, our proprietary DrainSmart® software, BIM integration, and close collaboration with clients from concept to completion.
What maintenance is required for Siphonic systems?+
Siphonic systems are known for low maintenance needs. Regular outlet inspections and periodic cleaning are recommended, but the self-cleaning mechanism of the siphonic action minimises the need for frequent interventions.
Is Siphonic Roof Drainage environmentally friendly?+
Yes. Siphonic systems contribute to water conservation by efficiently capturing and directing rainwater. They also reduce the quantity of materials required (fewer pipes, fewer outlets), lowering the overall construction carbon footprint.
