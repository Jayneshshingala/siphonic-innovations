— FREQUENTLY ASKED —

Got Questions?

What is Siphonic Roof Drainage? +
Siphonic Roof Drainage is an engineered rainwater management system that uses siphon action to rapidly drain roof areas without slope, using minimal pipe diameters and fewer downpipes — offering maximum efficiency and architectural flexibility.
How does Siphonic drainage differ from traditional gravity systems? +
Unlike gravity-based drainage, siphonic systems create a vacuum in the pipe network — enabling faster water removal, horizontal pipe routing without slope, and significantly fewer downpipes, especially effective over long horizontal distances.
What types of roofs are suitable for Siphonic drainage? +
Siphonic drainage is versatile: flat roofs, curved roofs, airport terminals, warehouses, industrial buildings, commercial developments, and large-span structures. Its adaptability makes it ideal across a wide range of architectural designs.
What are the main advantages of Siphonic Roof Drainage? +
Key benefits: efficient water removal, space optimisation (horizontal routing), cost-effectiveness, reduced material use, lower CO₂ footprint, architectural freedom, and minimal maintenance. Particularly advantageous for large roof areas and complex building structures.
How does Siphonic Innovations ensure project success? +
We combine 5+ years of hydraulic engineering experience with our proprietary DrainSmart® software, BIM integration, OEM-neutral product selection, and close collaboration with clients — from concept and design through to on-site execution support and post-handover maintenance.
What maintenance does a Siphonic system require? +
Siphonic systems are inherently low-maintenance. The high-velocity flow self-cleans pipe interiors. Regular outlet inspections and periodic cleaning of roof outlets are recommended. Our Commercial Maintenance team provides specialist siphonic servicing, gutter maintenance, roofing inspections, and drone surveys.
Is Siphonic Roof Drainage environmentally friendly? +
Yes. Siphonic systems require fewer pipes, fewer downpipes, and less material overall — directly reducing the construction carbon footprint. They can also be easily integrated with rainwater harvesting systems, supporting sustainable building goals.
